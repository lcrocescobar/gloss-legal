Gloss — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 18, 2026

Gloss is a skincare habit app. This policy explains what data the app handles and what happens to it.

Photos are never stored on our servers

When you take a skin scan, your photo is sent securely to our server, analyzed by an AI vision model to produce your scores, and immediately discarded. It is never saved, logged, or shared. A small thumbnail may be kept only on your device for your personal timeline; deleting the app deletes it.

What we do store

  • Scan scores — your daily glow score and the four metric scores (hydration, clarity, texture, radiance), plus the short text observations, so your history survives a reinstall or new phone.
  • Skin profile — your answers about skin feel, main concern, routine, SPF and makeup habits, used to personalize your analysis.
  • Account data — if you sign in, your email address (or Apple/Google identifier). First-time use may create an anonymous account with no personal information.
  • Usage counts — how many scans you run per day, to enforce fair-use limits.

Services we use

  • Supabase — authentication and database hosting for the data listed above.
  • Anthropic — the AI model that analyzes your scan photo. Photos are processed for the analysis only.
  • RevenueCat / Apple — subscription processing for Gloss+. We never see your payment details.

What we don't do

We do not sell your data, show ads, or use third-party advertising trackers. Your scores are yours.

Deleting your data

Settings → “Delete account & data” permanently deletes your account, scores, and profile from our servers, and clears everything stored on the device. You can also email us for deletion.

Children

Gloss is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.
